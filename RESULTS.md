## Primary claim

A stable Fe II log-cosine spectral-density mode in logarithmic wavenumber
coordinates `ell = ln(wavenumber_cm^-1)`. The signature is **bin-stability
of `k_best` across the 120 / 160 / 200 histogram-bin ladder**, with the
active-domain winding `n = k * Delta_ell / (2 pi)` clustered near 10.7.

`p_hat = (1 + tail_count_ge) / (1 + null_n)`. With zero null draws meeting
or exceeding the observed `DeltaD` at `null_n = 5000`, this gives
`p_hat = 1 / 5001 ≈ 0.00019996` — the smallest empirical p-value the
5000-null bootstrap can produce.

## Interpretation

- The result is a line-density structure in `ell = ln(sigma)`, not a flux
  spectrum.
- The headline is the *invariance* of `k_best` across binning, not the
  absolute size of `DeltaD` (which is bin-count dependent).
- `n_obs` near 10.7 is reported as the active-domain winding; nearby
  integer / Koide / folded targets are listed in each run's
  `nist_summary.json` under `branch_report.nearest`.

## Limitations

- Line-density scan over a public NIST Atomic Spectra Database CSV
  export — not a NIST-endorsed measurement.
- Per-`ell` line completeness varies; coverage is not uniform across
  the scan domain.
- The null is a parametric Poisson bootstrap from a Gaussian-smoothed
  baseline. Baseline-sigma sensitivity should be re-audited before
  extending the claim to other ions or species.
- The result has not been independently audited against alternative
  baseline models or non-parametric nulls.
